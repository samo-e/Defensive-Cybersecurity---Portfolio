## A12 – Discover 5 unique offline security tools

### Objective

Identify 5 different offline security tools used regularly. I researched some examples such as door locks, padlocks, CCTV, safes, and alarm systems that protect homes and businesses without needing internet access.

---

### Findings

* **Door locks**

  Door locks are one of the most basic and widely used security tools found in homes and offices. They use a physical key mechanism to lock and unlock doors, preventing unauthorised entry. Despite being simple, they provide an essential first layer of protection.

  I noticed that most houses still depend on normal locks more than people realise. Even though they are old-fashioned, a strong lock still matters.

* **Padlocks**

  These are portable locks that can be used on gates, lockers, bicycles, and storage units. They are easy to attach and remove, making them very flexible. Padlocks can use either a key or a combination code, depending on the type.

  In my view, padlocks are useful because they can be moved around easily. They are commonly seen in shared spaces where a fixed lock would not work.

* **CCTV cameras with offline recording**

  CCTV cameras with offline recording are used to monitor and record activities in homes, shops, and public spaces. These cameras store footage locally on a memory card or DVR system, allowing users to review incidents later even without internet access.

  I found this interesting because people often think CCTV must be online, but many systems work fine offline. Local recording is also better when internet is weak or not available.

* **Safes or security boxes**

  Safes or security boxes are used to store valuable items such as cash, jewellery, or important documents. They are made from strong materials and usually require a key or code to open, providing protection against theft.

  These are especially useful for passports and family documents. A safe does not stop all attacks, but it buys time and adds a strong barrier.

* **Alarm systems**

  Alarm systems are used in homes and businesses to detect unauthorised entry. When triggered, they produce a loud sound that alerts people nearby and can scare off intruders, acting as both a warning and deterrent.

  I have seen these in many shops and houses. Some systems also use sensors on windows and doors, which makes them more effective.

---

### Mitigations

Using strong tools, combining multiple layers of security, and maintaining devices regularly improves overall protection. For example, a lock plus an alarm is better than just one control alone.

It is also important to check batteries, replace worn parts, and test systems from time to time. A security tool is only useful if it actually works when needed, which people sometimes forget.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that simple physical tools play a major role in everyday security. Even without any internet connection, they help protect homes, property, and personal belongings.

I also realised that offline security is usually about delay and deterrence. These tools may not stop every attack, but they make it harder, louder, and riskier for an intruder to succeed.
