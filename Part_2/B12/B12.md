## B12 – Discover two bias cases when using a generative AI system

### Objective

The objective of this activity was to identify two real-world examples of bias in generative AI systems. The goal was to understand how AI models can sometimes produce unfair or biased outputs because of the data they are trained on.

---

### Findings

* One real-world case involved Amazon’s AI recruitment tool, which was designed to help review job applications. The system was later found to be biased against women because it had been trained on historical hiring data that mostly came from male applicants in the technology industry. As a result, the AI started penalising resumes that included words associated with women, such as “women’s chess club.” Amazon eventually stopped using the system after discovering the issue. This case showed how AI can unintentionally learn and repeat existing human biases from training data.

* Another real example involved Google’s AI image generation system, Google Gemini, which received criticism in 2024 after generating historically inaccurate and racially biased images in response to certain prompts. For example, users reported that the AI produced unrealistic representations of historical groups and events in an attempt to increase diversity in outputs. This caused public criticism because the generated images were seen as inaccurate and misleading. Google later paused parts of the image generation feature to address the issue.

---

### Mitigations

To reduce bias in AI systems, developers should use balanced and diverse datasets during training and regularly test models for unfair behaviour. Human oversight is also important because AI systems can still produce problematic outputs even after training. Transparency about how models are trained and improved can also help build trust and reduce harmful bias. In addition, feedback from users and external reviewers can help identify issues earlier.

---

### Reflection

This activity made me realise that AI systems are not automatically neutral or fair. Since they learn from human-created data, they can also inherit human biases and stereotypes. These real-world examples show that bias in AI can have serious consequences, especially in areas like hiring or public information. It also highlighted the importance of ethics and responsible development in AI systems.
