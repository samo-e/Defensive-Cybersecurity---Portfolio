## A5 – Discover cryptographic implementations used online

### Objective

The objective of this activity was to identify five different cryptographic implementations used online and understand how they help protect confidentiality, integrity, and trust in internet communication. I focused on common online services such as websites, messaging platforms, password systems, digital signatures, and online payment systems because these are the places where cryptography is used most often in daily life.

---

### Findings

* **HTTPS for secure web communication**

  HTTPS is used by websites to secure communication between the browser and the server. It relies on TLS to encrypt traffic so that sensitive data like passwords, personal details, and session cookies cannot be easily intercepted during transmission. In practice, this is one of the most common cryptographic methods used online.

  I noticed that HTTPS is especially important on login pages, banking portals, and shopping websites because it protects user data while it is travelling across the network. Without HTTPS, an attacker on the same network could potentially capture or even alter the traffic.

* **End-to-end encryption in messaging**

  End-to-end encryption is used in messaging services to protect message privacy. Under this model, only the sender and receiver can read the content of a message, while the service provider itself cannot directly access the plaintext. This is important because it reduces the risk of interception or unauthorised access.

  In my view, this is one of the strongest examples of applied cryptography because it protects not just the transmission channel but also the content itself. It is widely used in modern communication apps where privacy is a major concern.

* **Password hashing on servers**

  Password hashing is used to store passwords securely in online systems. Instead of keeping the actual password, the system stores a hashed version, which is a one-way cryptographic representation of the original input. This means that even if a database is leaked, the attacker does not automatically get the plaintext passwords.

  Strong password hashing also usually includes salting and slow hashing algorithms, which makes brute-force attacks much harder. This is a basic but very important security control for almost every online account system.

* **Digital signatures for authenticity**

  Digital signatures are used to verify the origin and integrity of online data. They help confirm that a message, document, or software package has not been changed after signing and that it was created by the claimed sender. This makes them useful for email security, software updates, and legally important digital documents.

  I found this concept important because it addresses trust as well as confidentiality. Even if the data is not secret, users still need confidence that it is genuine and unchanged.

* **Tokenisation in online payments**

  Secure online payment systems use encryption and tokenisation to protect financial data. In tokenisation, sensitive card information is replaced with a non-sensitive token that can be used in place of the original details. This reduces exposure because merchants do not need to store the actual card number in many cases.

  This is particularly relevant for online shopping and subscription services, where payment credentials may be reused frequently. Tokenisation works well alongside encryption and authentication to reduce the risk of fraud.

---

### Mitigations

To maintain strong cryptographic security, online systems should use modern standards such as TLS 1.3, secure hashing algorithms, and strong key management practices. Private keys should be stored securely, and systems should be updated regularly so that old or weak cryptographic methods are not left in use.

It is also important to combine cryptography with other controls such as multi-factor authentication, access control, and secure password policies. Cryptography is powerful, but it works best as part of a layered security approach rather than as a standalone solution.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that cryptography is essential for secure online communication and transactions. It protects user data in ways that are often invisible to users, but it is still one of the most important parts of modern internet security.

I also realised that different cryptographic techniques solve different problems. HTTPS protects data in transit, password hashing protects stored credentials, digital signatures provide trust, and tokenisation reduces payment risk. This made it clear that cryptography is not a single tool, but a group of techniques used for different security goals.
