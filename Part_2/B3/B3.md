## B3 – Discover 3 proactive security implementations in practice

### Objective

The objective of this activity was to identify three proactive security implementations used in real-world environments. The goal was to understand how organisations and individuals try to prevent security incidents before they happen instead of only reacting after an attack occurs.

---

### Findings

* One common proactive security implementation is the use of **regular software updates and patch management**. Many organisations automatically update their systems and applications to fix known vulnerabilities before attackers can exploit them. This is considered proactive because the security issue is addressed before it becomes an actual incident.

* Another proactive implementation is the use of **security awareness training** for employees and users. Companies often conduct phishing simulations, awareness sessions, and cybersecurity training to teach people how to recognise suspicious emails, unsafe links, and social engineering attacks. Since human error is a major cause of breaches, educating users helps reduce risks early.

* Continuous **network monitoring and intrusion detection systems** are also proactive security measures. These systems constantly monitor network activity and look for unusual behaviour or attack patterns. Instead of waiting for damage to occur, they can alert administrators early and allow them to respond before the situation becomes more serious.

---

### Mitigations

To improve proactive security, organisations should regularly review and update their security policies, maintain updated systems, and continue training users about new threats. Monitoring systems should also be configured properly to reduce false alarms and improve detection accuracy. Combining technical protections with user awareness creates a stronger overall security posture.

---

### Reflection

This activity made me realise that proactive security is much more effective than only reacting after an attack happens. Preventing incidents early usually saves time, money, and damage compared to responding later. It also showed that proactive security is not only about technology, but also about planning, monitoring, and educating people continuously.
