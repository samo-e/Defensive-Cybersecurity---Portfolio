## A3 – Discover security concepts used in your house

### Objective

Identify different security concepts used in a residential environment. The goal is to understand how everyday systems at home help in protecting people, property, and access to restricted areas.

---

### Findings

* CCTV cameras are installed in common lobby areas of the building, which help monitor movement and activity. These cameras continuously record footage, which can be used later if any suspicious activity or incident occurs. Even just having cameras visible can discourage people from doing anything wrong.

* A number keypad lock is used for lift access from the basement, which acts as an access control system. Only people who know the correct code can use the lift from that level, which prevents random individuals from entering the residential floors directly from the basement.

* The basement garage entry is restricted using a remote control that is only given to residents who own a car. This means that only authorised people can open the garage gate, reducing the chances of unauthorised vehicles or people entering the building.

---

### Mitigations

Security in the house can be improved by regularly checking that CCTV cameras are working properly and storing recordings safely. Access codes for the keypad lock should be changed occasionally to reduce the risk of sharing or guessing. Garage remotes should be managed carefully, and lost or stolen remotes should be deactivated quickly. Combining these systems with basic awareness from residents can make the overall security stronger.

---

### Reflection

This task made me realise that security concepts are already being used in everyday living spaces without us thinking much about them. Simple systems like cameras, keypads, and remotes all work together to create a safer environment. It also shows that even basic systems can be effective if they are used and managed properly.
