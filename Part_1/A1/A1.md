## A1 – Discover security concepts used on UWA campus

### Objective

Identify different security concepts used on the UWA campus. The goal is to understand how the university maintains safety and protects students, staff, and facilities.

---

### Findings

* CCTV cameras are installed across different parts of the UWA campus, including lecture halls, libraries, and walkways. These cameras help monitor activity and can be used to review incidents if something happens. Just having cameras around also makes people think twice before doing anything wrong.

* Student ID card access is used in certain buildings and rooms, especially after hours. Only students or staff with valid cards can enter, which helps restrict access to authorised people and keeps facilities more secure.

* Security patrols are carried out by campus security staff, especially during the evening and night. Their presence helps respond quickly to any issues and also acts as a deterrent for unwanted behaviour.

* Emergency help points (blue light phones) are placed around campus, allowing students to quickly contact security if they feel unsafe. This adds an extra layer of safety, especially in quieter areas.

* Lighting across the campus is well designed, especially along pathways and near buildings. Good lighting improves visibility at night and reduces the chances of unsafe situations.

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

Campus security can be improved by regularly maintaining cameras and emergency systems to make sure they are always working. Access control systems should be updated and monitored to prevent misuse of ID cards. Increasing awareness among students about available safety features can also help. Overall, combining these systems with active monitoring makes the campus safer.

---

### Reflection

This task made me realise that a lot of security measures are already in place on campus, but we usually don’t pay attention to them. Things like cameras, ID access, and patrols all work together to create a safer environment. It also shows that security is not just one system, but a combination of different ideas working together.
