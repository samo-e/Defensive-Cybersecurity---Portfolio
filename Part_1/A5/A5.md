## A5 – Discover cryptographic implementations used online

### Objective

Identify five different cryptographic implementations used online. The goal is to understand how encryption protects data and communication on the internet.

---

### Findings

* HTTPS is used by websites to secure communication between the user and the server. It encrypts data using SSL/TLS, ensuring that sensitive information like passwords cannot be easily intercepted during transmission.

* End-to-end encryption is used in messaging services to ensure that only the sender and receiver can read messages. Even the service provider cannot access the content, which improves privacy.

* Password hashing is used to store passwords securely on servers. Instead of storing the actual password, a hashed version is stored, making it harder for attackers to retrieve original passwords.

* Digital signatures are used to verify the authenticity of data or messages. They ensure that the data has not been altered and confirm the identity of the sender.

* Secure online payment systems use encryption and tokenisation to protect financial data. Sensitive details like card numbers are either encrypted or replaced with tokens to reduce exposure.

---

### Mitigations

To maintain strong cryptographic security, it is important to use modern encryption standards and keep systems updated. Private keys should be stored securely, and multiple layers of security such as authentication and encryption should be combined to reduce risks.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that cryptography is essential for secure online communication and transactions. It plays a key role in protecting user data even though users may not directly see it.
