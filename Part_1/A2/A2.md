## A2 – Discover security concepts used in public space

### Objective

The objective of this activity was to identify five security concepts used in public spaces and understand how they contribute to safety, deterrence, and incident response. I focused on public spaces in Perth, Western Australia, such as Transperth stations, the Perth CBD, and Perth Airport, because these are familiar local environments where security controls are visible in everyday life.

---

### Findings

- **CCTV surveillance**

  CCTV is widely used in public spaces in Perth to monitor activity and support quick response to incidents. The Public Transport Authority uses CCTV across train stations, bus stations, buses, trains, ferries, and car parks, with footage monitored 24/7 by a central monitoring room. The City of Perth also uses CCTV across the CBD, which shows that surveillance is a key part of public safety management in the city.

  In practice, CCTV helps in two ways: it discourages unwanted behaviour because people know they may be recorded, and it provides evidence after an incident occurs. This makes it one of the most visible and practical security concepts in public spaces.

- **Access control in restricted areas**

  Public spaces are not completely open; they often include restricted areas where access must be controlled. At Perth Airport, secure and airside zones are controlled through screening, restricted entry, and identification-based access rules. This prevents unauthorised people from entering sensitive areas such as baggage handling zones, boarding areas, or staff-only sections.

  This is an important security concept because it shows that access control is used even in environments that appear public. In other words, public spaces often have hidden layers of restriction behind the visible area that the public sees.

- **Security screening**

  Security screening is a strong preventive measure used in airports and other high-risk public environments. Perth Airport has upgraded its screening infrastructure with body scanners, walk-through metal detectors, X-ray systems, and explosive trace detection tools. These systems inspect passengers and luggage before entry, reducing the chance of dangerous items entering secure areas.

  Screening is effective because it stops threats before they enter the protected environment. Compared with response-only measures, it is more proactive and better suited to places with high passenger movement.

- **Visible security presence**

  A visible security presence is another major concept used in public spaces. In Perth’s public transport network, transit officers, security staff, and central monitoring officers work together to patrol stations and respond to incidents. The PTA notes that specially trained transit officers patrol the network, while staff in the monitoring room operate 24/7 to observe incidents and coordinate responses.

  This presence is important because it creates deterrence and also improves response time. When people can see officers or security staff nearby, they are less likely to behave aggressively or commit vandalism.

- **Environmental design and lighting**

  Lighting is a basic but very effective public-space security concept. The City of Perth has a lighting strategy focused on improving safety, night-time movement, and the overall quality of public areas. Better lighting reduces hidden corners, improves visibility for pedestrians and CCTV, and makes public spaces feel safer after dark.

  In my opinion, lighting is often underestimated because it looks simple, but it supports both prevention and surveillance. If a place is dark, even strong CCTV or patrol presence becomes less effective.

---

### Mitigations

Public safety in Perth public spaces is improved when several security concepts work together instead of relying on one control only. CCTV, access control, screening, patrols, emergency communication, and lighting should be treated as a layered defence model, because each one covers a different risk.

A practical improvement would be regular maintenance of cameras, lights, and screening equipment so that blind spots, technical failures, and poor visibility are reduced. Another useful measure is better signage and public awareness, because people are more likely to report suspicious behaviour when they know who to contact and where emergency devices are located.

For transport hubs and airports, security should also be reviewed periodically based on passenger flow, crowd density, and local incident patterns. This is important because public spaces change over time, and security measures that were adequate earlier may become less effective if the environment becomes busier or more complex.

---

### Reflection

This activity showed me that public-space security is not just about stopping crime after it happens. It is about creating a layered environment where surveillance, screening, restricted access, patrol presence, and lighting all work together to reduce risk.

The Perth examples made the concept easier to understand because they show how security is implemented in real places that people use daily, such as train stations, bus interchanges, the CBD, and the airport. My main learning is that a good public-space security system should be visible, practical, and continuously maintained rather than being treated as a one-time installation.
