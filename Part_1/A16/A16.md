## A16 – Discover 3 local security incidents

### Objective

Identify three recent security incidents that occurred in Perth. The goal is to understand real-world security issues at a local level and how they impact people and organisations.

---

### Findings

* A major cyber security incident happened at the University of Western Australia, where thousands of staff and student passwords were exposed due to unauthorised access to their systems. As a response, the university had to lock everyone out and force password resets to contain the situation. Even though no other data was confirmed to be leaked, it still shows how sensitive information like passwords can be targeted and how disruptive these attacks can be. ([ABC News][1])

* A Perth-based company called Intellect Systems was reportedly targeted by a ransomware attack from a group known as Akira. The attackers claimed to have stolen around 10GB of sensitive data, including personal and company information like IDs and financial details. This incident highlights how businesses, especially those linked to infrastructure, can be targeted through vulnerabilities in their systems. ([Cyber News Centre][2])

* A serious physical security incident occurred during an Invasion Day event at Forrest Place, where a man threw a homemade explosive device into a large crowd. Luckily, the device failed to detonate, but it had the potential to cause major harm. The incident is being treated as a possible terrorist act and shows how public events can become targets if security is not strong enough. ([Wikipedia][3])

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

To reduce the risk of similar incidents, organisations should strengthen cybersecurity measures such as regular system updates, strong password policies, and monitoring for unusual activity. Businesses should also secure their networks properly to prevent ransomware attacks. For public safety, better surveillance, crowd monitoring, and threat detection at large events can help prevent or respond to dangerous situations more effectively. Overall, both digital and physical security need to be taken seriously together.

### Reflection

This task made me realise that security incidents are not just something that happens globally but also locally in places like Perth. Both cyber attacks and physical threats can have serious consequences. It also shows that even well-known organisations and public events are not completely safe, which makes proper security planning really important.
