## A2 – Discover security concepts used in public space

### Objective

Identify five different security concepts used in public spaces (e.g., malls, airports, streets). The goal is to understand how safety is maintained using different security strategies and systems.

---

### Method

1. Defined scope:

* Focused on public places such as shopping centres, transport stations, and streets.
* Included both physical and procedural security concepts.

2. Research approach:

* Observed common security measures in public areas.
* Selected concepts that are widely used and easy to understand.

3. Selection criteria:

* Must be used in public spaces.
* Each concept must represent a different security idea.

---

### Findings

#### 1. Surveillance (CCTV Monitoring)

* Used in malls, streets, and transport stations.

**How it works:**

* Cameras record activities in public areas.
* Security staff monitor live or recorded footage.

**Weakness:**

* Does not stop crime directly, mainly helps after it happens.

---

#### 2. Access Control

* Used in restricted areas (staff-only zones, airports).

**How it works:**

* Only authorised people can enter certain areas using cards, tickets, or checks.

**Weakness:**

* Can fail if credentials are shared or stolen.

---

#### 3. Security Screening

* Used in airports, events, and government buildings.

**How it works:**

* People and bags are checked using scanners or manual inspection.

**Weakness:**

* Can cause delays and may miss hidden threats.

---

#### 4. Visible Security Presence

* Includes guards and police in public areas.

**How it works:**

* Acts as a deterrent to crime.
* Guards can respond quickly to incidents.

**Weakness:**

* Limited coverage; cannot be everywhere at once.

---

#### 5. Lighting (Environmental Design)

* Used in streets, parking areas, and parks.

**How it works:**

* Bright lighting reduces hiding spots and increases visibility.

**Weakness:**

* Ineffective if lights are broken or poorly placed.

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

1. Combine multiple concepts:

* Example: CCTV + guards + access control.

2. Regular maintenance:

* Ensure cameras, lights, and systems are working.

3. Train security staff:

* Proper training improves response and effectiveness.

4. Public awareness:

* Encourage people to report suspicious activity.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that public safety relies on a combination of different security concepts. No single method is enough on its own. Visible measures like cameras and guards help prevent crime, while systems like access control and screening provide extra protection. Good security design makes public spaces safer for everyone.
