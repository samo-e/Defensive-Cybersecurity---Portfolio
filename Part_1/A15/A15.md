## A15 – Discover 5 recent security incidents

### Objective

Identify five recent real-world security incidents. The goal is to understand how cyberattacks happen in practice and what kind of impact they have on organisations and users.

---

### Findings

* A recent data breach involving Mazda exposed employee and partner information after attackers gained unauthorised access to one of its systems. The breach affected hundreds of individuals and included details like names and email addresses. It happened due to system vulnerabilities, showing how even large companies can be affected if systems are not properly secured. ([TechRadar][1])

* Crunchyroll was involved in a potential data breach where hackers claimed to have accessed over 100GB of sensitive data through a third-party service provider. This included user information like emails and possibly payment details. It highlights how third-party dependencies can create security risks even if the main company systems are secure. ([GamesRadar+][2])

* A major cyberattack targeted Poland’s energy infrastructure in 2025, likely carried out by a state-linked group. The attackers used destructive malware, and although power supply was not fully disrupted, the attack showed how critical infrastructure can be targeted to cause serious damage and instability. ([AP News][3])

* A vulnerability in VMware ESXi systems was actively exploited by ransomware attackers in 2026. Attackers were able to gain deep access to virtual environments, which increased the risk of data theft and system takeover. This incident shows how unpatched software vulnerabilities can quickly be used by attackers. ([CM Alliance][4])

* The Qantas data breach in 2025 affected millions of customers, with personal information such as names and travel details being exposed. The breach involved a third-party platform, again showing that weak security in connected systems can lead to large-scale data exposure. ([New Era Technology][5])

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

To reduce the risk of similar incidents, organisations should regularly update and patch their systems to fix known vulnerabilities. Strong security controls should also be applied to third-party vendors, as many attacks happen through external systems. Using encryption, multi-factor authentication, and continuous monitoring can help detect and prevent attacks earlier. In general, having a proper incident response plan also helps reduce damage when a breach does happen.

---

### Reflection

This task made me realise that cyberattacks are happening very frequently and can affect any type of organisation, from entertainment platforms to critical infrastructure. A common pattern I noticed is that many attacks happen due to weak points like outdated systems or third-party services. It also shows that security is not just about protecting one system, but the whole ecosystem connected to it.
