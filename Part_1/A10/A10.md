## A10 – Discover privacy techniques used offline

### Objective

The objective of this activity was to identify five different offline privacy techniques used in daily life to protect personal information from physical exposure. I focused on common methods such as document disposal, physical locks, screen protection, PIN entry habits, and conversation awareness because these are practical ways to maintain privacy without technology.

---

### Findings

* **Shredding sensitive documents**

  Shredding documents helps prevent sensitive information from being recovered after disposal by cutting paper into small pieces. This is important for things like bank statements, ID copies, or medical records that could be used for identity theft if found in the trash.

  I noticed that cross-cut shredders are better than strip-cut because they make reconstruction much harder. Many people still just throw papers away without thinking about it.

* **Locking personal items**

  Locking personal items in drawers or safes protects private documents from unauthorised access. A small home safe or locked filing cabinet keeps passports, financial papers, and personal letters secure even if someone enters your home or office.

  In my view, this is basic but effective, especially for renters or shared living spaces. Combination locks are good because they don't need keys that can be lost.

* **Privacy screens on devices**

  Privacy screens limit viewing angles on devices, preventing others from seeing the screen in public places. These thin films make the display visible only when looking straight on, which is useful on laptops or phones in cafes or trains.

  This technique is great for working in public because shoulder surfing is common. They are affordable and easy to attach, though they can make the screen a bit darker.

* **Covering keypads for PIN entry**

  Covering keypads while entering PINs helps prevent others from observing sensitive information. When using an ATM or keypad lock, using your hand or body to block the view stops people from memorising your code.

  I found this important because many breaches happen from simple observation. It is a quick habit that costs nothing but makes a big difference.

* **Managing conversations for privacy**

  Speaking in private or lowering voice protects conversations from being overheard. Choosing quiet locations, avoiding sensitive topics in public, or using code words helps keep personal discussions confidential.

  This is often overlooked, but cafes and public transport are full of people listening. Being aware of surroundings prevents accidental leaks.

---

### Mitigations

Using multiple techniques together, staying aware of surroundings, and developing good habits can significantly improve offline privacy protection. For example, shredding plus locking plus keypad covering creates layers of defence.

It is also good to educate family or roommates about privacy. Sometimes the biggest risk is people close to you who don't realise they are exposing information.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that privacy protection is not only digital but also depends on everyday physical actions and awareness. Simple habits like shredding or covering PINs can prevent many problems.

I also realised that offline privacy requires constant vigilance. Unlike online tools, there is no "enable privacy mode" — you have to think about it every time.
