## A12 – Discover 5 unique offline security tools

### Objective

Identify five offline security tools used in everyday life.

---

### Findings

* Door locks are one of the most basic and widely used security tools found in homes and offices. They use a physical key mechanism to lock and unlock doors, preventing unauthorised entry. Despite being simple, they provide an essential first layer of protection.

* Padlocks are portable locks that can be used on gates, lockers, bicycles, and storage units. They are easy to attach and remove, making them very flexible. Padlocks can use either a key or a combination code, depending on the type.

* CCTV cameras with offline recording are used to monitor and record activities in homes, shops, and public spaces. These cameras store footage locally on a memory card or DVR system, allowing users to review incidents later even without internet access.

* Safes or security boxes are used to store valuable items such as cash, jewellery, or important documents. They are made from strong materials and usually require a key or code to open, providing protection against theft.

* Alarm systems are used in homes and businesses to detect unauthorised entry. When triggered, they produce a loud sound that alerts people nearby and can scare off intruders, acting as both a warning and deterrent.

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

Using strong tools, combining multiple layers of security, and maintaining devices regularly improves overall protection.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that simple physical tools play a major role in everyday security.
