**A5 – Discover Cryptographic Implementations Used Online**

**Objective**
The aim of this task was to identify and understand five common cryptographic implementations used in real-world online systems, focusing on how they secure data, communication, and user identity.

**Method**
The research focused on practical, real-world applications of cryptography across websites, messaging platforms, and online payment systems. Each example was selected to represent a different cryptographic technique and its specific purpose in securing online interactions.

**Findings**

1. **HTTPS (SSL/TLS Encryption)**
   HTTPS is widely used to secure communication between users and websites such as Google, Amazon, and banking portals. It encrypts data exchanged between the browser and server using SSL/TLS protocols and verifies website identity through digital certificates. However, improper certificate configuration can weaken security.

2. **End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)**
   E2EE is used in messaging applications like WhatsApp and Signal. Messages are encrypted on the sender’s device and can only be decrypted by the intended receiver. This ensures privacy even from the service provider. A limitation is that if a user’s device is compromised, the encrypted messages can still be accessed.

3. **Password Hashing**
   Websites like Facebook and Instagram use password hashing to securely store user credentials. Instead of storing passwords in plain text, they are converted into hashes using algorithms such as bcrypt or SHA variants. During login, the system compares hashes. Weak hashing algorithms, however, can be vulnerable to attacks.

4. **Digital Signatures**
   Digital signatures are used to verify authenticity and integrity of data. For example, software updates from companies like Microsoft or Apple are digitally signed to ensure they are not tampered with. The sender signs data with a private key, and the receiver verifies it using a public key. If the private key is stolen, signatures can be forged.

5. **Secure Online Payments (Encryption and Tokenization)**
   Online payment platforms like PayPal and Stripe use encryption along with tokenization to protect card details. Sensitive information is either encrypted or replaced with tokens, reducing exposure during transactions. The effectiveness of this system depends on the security practices of the payment provider.

**Mitigations**
To enhance security, modern encryption algorithms should be used, systems must be regularly updated, private keys must be securely stored, and multiple layers of security (such as authentication and encryption) should be implemented.

**Reflection**
This study highlights how cryptography is essential in everyday online activities. From browsing websites to sending messages and making payments, cryptographic techniques ensure confidentiality, integrity, and authentication. Proper implementation is critical to maintaining strong and reliable security.
