## A29 – Find a publicly available AI-generated image, video, or audio clip, and analyse it

### Objective

Find a real example of AI-generated media and analyse it using at least one detection or verification tool. The goal is to understand how AI-generated content can be identified and what signs or tools can help verify it.

---

### Findings

* A well-known example of an AI-generated image is the viral “Balenciaga Pope” image, which showed Pope Francis wearing a stylish white puffer jacket. The image spread widely online and many people initially believed it was real because of how realistic it looked. However, it was later confirmed to be AI-generated and became a popular example of how convincing synthetic images can be. ([pope-francis-ai-jacket.jpg-295135-scaled.webp][1])

* To analyse this image, an AI detection tool such as DeepAI Image Detector can be used. These tools work by analysing patterns in the image such as texture, lighting, and pixel inconsistencies to determine if it was generated by AI. When similar AI-generated images are tested, these tools often return a high probability (for example, around 80–90%) that the image is fake. ([DeepAI][2])

* When analysing the image more closely (even without tools), there are small inconsistencies that can be noticed. For example, details like the hands, fingers, and fabric textures may look slightly unrealistic or distorted. AI-generated images often struggle with fine details, symmetry, and reflections, which are common signs used by detection tools as well.

* When the picture was run through the DeepAI Image Detector tool, the result was as expected. DeepAI presented a 97% AI likelihood on the image. ([DeepAI_image_result.png][3])

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

To reduce the risk of being misled by AI-generated content, it is important to verify suspicious images using multiple detection tools instead of relying on just one. Users should also look closely at visual details like hands, text, and reflections, as these are common areas where AI makes mistakes. Reverse image search and checking trusted news sources can also help confirm whether an image is real. Overall, being slightly sceptical of viral content and taking a few extra steps to verify it can prevent misinformation.
