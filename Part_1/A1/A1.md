## A1 – Discover security concepts used on campus

### Objective

Identify different security concepts used on the University of Western Australia (UWA) campus. The goal is to understand how a large university environment maintains safety for students, staff, and visitors.

---

### Findings

* Surveillance through CCTV cameras is widely used across the UWA campus, including areas like libraries and common spaces. These cameras allow security staff to monitor activity in real time and review footage if needed. For example, UWA libraries have extensive CCTV installed to monitor spaces and improve safety.

* Security patrols and physical presence are an important concept used on campus. UWA has security officers available 24/7 who patrol the campus, respond to incidents, and assist students when needed. This visible presence helps prevent incidents and ensures quick response in emergencies.

* Access control is used in buildings and systems to restrict entry to authorised users only. UWA uses systems like UniID login credentials for digital access and controlled building entry for certain areas, ensuring that only approved individuals can access sensitive spaces or systems.

* Emergency response systems are another key concept, including tools like the SafeZone app. This app allows students and staff to quickly contact campus security with a single button, which improves response time during emergencies and increases overall safety.

* Security escort services are provided for students, especially after hours. Students can request a security officer to escort them across campus, for example to car parks or accommodation, which reduces the risk of unsafe situations during late hours.

* Network security and segmentation is also used behind the scenes to protect digital systems. UWA uses secure network design to control access to different parts of its systems and protect sensitive data.

---

### Mitigations

Security on campus can be improved by regularly maintaining CCTV systems and ensuring cameras cover all important areas. Students should be encouraged to actively use tools like the SafeZone app instead of ignoring them. Access control systems should be updated and monitored to prevent misuse, and awareness programs can help students understand how to stay safe on campus. Regular security reviews and updates to both physical and digital systems can further strengthen overall protection.

---

### Reflection

This task made me realise that campus security is not just one system but a combination of many different concepts working together. From cameras and guards to apps and network security, everything plays a role in keeping students safe. It also showed that a lot of these systems are already in place, but students don’t always notice them or use them properly.
