## A9 – Discover privacy techniques used online

### Objective

The objective of this activity was to identify five different privacy techniques used online to protect personal information and user activity. I focused on common methods such as VPNs, authentication, passwords, privacy settings, and browsing modes because these are practical ways individuals can improve their online privacy in daily use.

---

### Findings

* **VPN for traffic encryption and IP hiding**

  Using a VPN helps protect user privacy by encrypting internet traffic and hiding the user’s IP address, making it harder to track online activity. This is useful when using public Wi-Fi or accessing geo-restricted content without revealing your location.

  I noticed that VPNs are popular for streaming and browsing because they prevent ISPs or websites from seeing what you are doing. However, free VPNs sometimes log data, so paid ones are better for real privacy.

* **Two-factor authentication (2FA)**

  Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification step, reducing the risk of account compromise. Even if someone gets your password, they cannot log in without the second factor like a code from your phone.

  In my view, this is one of the easiest privacy techniques because it is supported by most services now. It makes phishing attacks much less effective.

* **Strong and unique passwords**

  Using strong and unique passwords for different accounts prevents attackers from accessing multiple services if one password is leaked. A password manager can help generate and store these without needing to remember them all.

  This is important because many people reuse passwords across sites. If one site is breached, unique passwords limit the damage to just that account.

* **Privacy settings on websites and apps**

  Adjusting privacy settings on websites allows users to control what information is shared publicly and with third parties. For example, you can limit who sees your posts or turn off data collection for ads.

  I found this technique useful because default settings often share more than needed. Checking them regularly helps reduce unwanted tracking.

* **Private browsing mode**

  Private browsing mode prevents the browser from storing history, cookies, and temporary data on the device. This is good for using a shared computer because it does not leave traces of what sites you visited.

  However, it does not hide your activity from websites or ISPs, so it is more about local privacy than complete anonymity.

---

### Mitigations

Online privacy can be improved by combining multiple techniques such as VPNs, strong passwords, and 2FA. Users should regularly review privacy settings and avoid sharing sensitive information unnecessarily.

It is also good to use browser extensions that block trackers and ads. Being aware of what data you share and why makes a big difference in maintaining privacy over time.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that protecting privacy online requires both tools and responsible user behaviour. No single technique is perfect, but using several together creates better protection.

I also realised that privacy is an ongoing process. Services change their policies, and new tracking methods appear, so staying informed and adjusting habits is important.
