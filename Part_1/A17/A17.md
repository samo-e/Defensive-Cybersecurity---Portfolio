## A17 – Discover 10 different types of locks in use

### Objective

Identify ten different types of physical locks used in daily life. The goal is to understand how different lock designs provide security for homes, buildings, and personal property.

---

### Method

1. Defined scope:

* Focused only on physical (non-digital) locks.
* Included locks used in homes, businesses, and personal items.

2. Research approach:

* Observed common locks used in real life (doors, lockers, gates, etc.).
* Selected locks with different mechanisms and designs.

3. Selection criteria:

* Each lock must be physically different.
* Each must use a different locking mechanism or purpose.

---

### Findings

#### 1. Deadbolt Lock

* Commonly used on main doors.

**How it works:**

* A solid metal bolt extends into the door frame using a key.

**Weakness:**

* Can be picked or forced if low quality.

---

#### 2. Padlock

* Used for gates, lockers, and chains.

**How it works:**

* A portable lock with a shackle that opens with a key or code.

**Weakness:**

* Can be cut with bolt cutters if not strong.

---

#### 3. Knob Lock

* Found on interior doors.

**How it works:**

* Lock is built into the door knob.

**Weakness:**

* Easy to break with force.

---

#### 4. Lever Handle Lock

* Used in offices and commercial buildings.

**How it works:**

* Uses a lever instead of a knob to open the door.

**Weakness:**

* Can wear out over time.

---

#### 5. Mortise Lock

* Used in older or high-security doors.

**How it works:**

* Installed inside the door with a complex internal mechanism.

**Weakness:**

* More expensive and harder to install.

---

#### 6. Cam Lock

* Used in cabinets, drawers, and mailboxes.

**How it works:**

* A rotating metal piece (cam) locks or unlocks.

**Weakness:**

* Not very strong; can be forced open.

---

#### 7. Rim Lock

* Mounted on the surface of a door.

**How it works:**

* Locking mechanism is visible on the inside of the door.

**Weakness:**

* Less secure than internal locks.

---

#### 8. Cylinder Lock (Euro Cylinder)

* Common in modern doors.

**How it works:**

* Uses a cylinder that turns with a key to lock/unlock.

**Weakness:**

* Can be snapped or picked if low quality.

---

#### 9. Chain Lock

* Used on doors for partial opening.

**How it works:**

* A chain allows the door to open slightly while still locked.

**Weakness:**

* Can be broken with force.

---

#### 10. Combination Lock

* Used on lockers and safes.

**How it works:**

* Opens using a number code instead of a key.

**Weakness:**

* Code can be guessed or observed.

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

1. Use high-quality locks:

* Strong materials reduce the risk of breaking.

2. Combine multiple locks:

* Example: deadbolt + chain lock for extra security.

3. Regular maintenance:

* Ensure locks are working properly.

4. Keep keys and codes safe:

* Avoid sharing access with others.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that there are many types of physical locks, each designed for different situations. Some locks are strong and used for high security, while others are simple and used for convenience. No single lock is perfect, so using multiple locks together can provide better protection.
