## A5 – Discover cryptographic implementations used online

### Objective

Identify five different cryptographic implementations used online. The goal is to understand how encryption and cryptography are used to protect data, communication, and user identity on the internet.

---

### Method

1. Defined scope:

* Focused on real-world uses of cryptography in online systems.
* Included both communication and data protection methods.

2. Research approach:

* Looked at common online services (websites, messaging apps, payments).
* Selected implementations that use different cryptographic techniques.

3. Selection criteria:

* Must be used online.
* Each example must show a different use of cryptography.

---

### Findings

#### 1. HTTPS (SSL/TLS Encryption)

* Used by websites to secure communication.

**How it works:**

* Encrypts data between the user’s browser and the website.
* Uses certificates to verify the website identity.

**Weakness:**

* Misconfigured certificates can reduce security.

---

#### 2. End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)

* Used in messaging apps.

**How it works:**

* Messages are encrypted on the sender’s device.
* Only the receiver can decrypt them.

**Weakness:**

* If a device is compromised, messages can still be accessed.

---

#### 3. Password Hashing (e.g., hashing algorithms)

* Used to store passwords securely.

**How it works:**

* Passwords are converted into a hash (not stored as plain text).
* System compares hashes during login.

**Weakness:**

* Weak hashing methods can be cracked.

---

#### 4. Digital Signatures

* Used for verifying authenticity of data.

**How it works:**

* A sender signs data using a private key.
* Receiver verifies it using a public key.

**Weakness:**

* If private key is stolen, signatures can be forged.

---

#### 5. Secure Online Payments (Encryption + Tokenization)

* Used in online shopping and banking.

**How it works:**

* Card details are encrypted or replaced with tokens.
* Reduces exposure of sensitive data.

**Weakness:**

* Depends on the security of the payment provider.

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

1. Use strong algorithms:

* Prefer modern encryption standards.

2. Keep systems updated:

* Fix vulnerabilities in cryptographic libraries.

3. Protect private keys:

* Store keys securely and limit access.

4. Use multi-layer security:

* Combine encryption with authentication methods.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that cryptography is a key part of online security. It protects data, verifies identity, and ensures safe communication. While users may not see it directly, it is used in many everyday online activities like browsing, messaging, and payments. Proper implementation is important to maintain strong security.
