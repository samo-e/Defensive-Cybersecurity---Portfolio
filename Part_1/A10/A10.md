## A10 – Discover privacy techniques used offline

### Objective

Identify five different privacy techniques used offline (without internet). The goal is to understand how people protect their personal information and privacy in everyday physical environments.

---

### Method

1. Defined scope:

* Focused on privacy techniques used in daily life without internet.
* Included both personal habits and physical tools.

2. Research approach:

* Observed common practices in homes, offices, and public places.
* Selected techniques that help prevent others from seeing or accessing private information.

3. Selection criteria:

* Must work offline.
* Each technique must protect privacy in a different way.

---

### Findings

#### 1. Shredding Documents

* Used for disposing of sensitive papers (bills, bank statements).

**How it works:**

* Paper is cut into small pieces using a shredder.

**Weakness:**

* Basic shredders can sometimes be reconstructed.

---

#### 2. Locking Personal Items (Drawers/Safes)

* Used to store private documents or valuables.

**How it works:**

* Items are kept in locked spaces to prevent access.

**Weakness:**

* Keys can be lost or stolen.

---

#### 3. Using Privacy Screens (Laptop/Phone)

* Used in public places like libraries or cafes.

**How it works:**

* A screen filter limits viewing angles.
* Only the person directly in front can see the screen.

**Weakness:**

* Does not stop someone directly behind.

---

#### 4. Covering Keypads (e.g., ATM PIN Entry)

* Used when entering sensitive information.

**How it works:**

* Hand is used to block others from seeing the keypad.

**Weakness:**

* Cameras may still capture input.

---

#### 5. Sound Privacy (Lowering Voice / Private Rooms)

* Used during private conversations.

**How it works:**

* Speaking quietly or moving to a private space to avoid being overheard.

**Weakness:**

* Not effective in very crowded or noisy environments.

---

### Mitigations (high-level recommendations)

1. Combine techniques:

* Example: shred documents and store important ones in a safe.

2. Stay aware of surroundings:

* Be careful in public places when handling private information.

3. Use better tools:

* Cross-cut shredders and strong locks provide better protection.

4. Develop good habits:

* Privacy depends a lot on user behaviour.

---

### Reflection

This task showed that privacy is not only about technology but also about everyday actions. Simple techniques like shredding papers or covering a keypad can make a big difference. Protecting privacy offline requires awareness and consistent habits, not just tools.
