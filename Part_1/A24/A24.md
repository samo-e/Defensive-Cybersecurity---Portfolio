## A24 – Teach your family about a cybersecurity topic

For this activity, I conducted a short session with my mother where I explained some basic everyday cybersecurity awareness. The focus was not on anything technical, but more on simple habits that can actually prevent most common online problems. I used a small presentation to guide the session.

The main topics I covered included using strong passwords, being careful with suspicious links, keeping devices updated, and knowing when to stop and double-check something. For example, I explained that many scams try to create urgency, like saying something is wrong with your bank account or asking for money quickly, and that these are common warning signs. 

I also showed her some practical habits she can follow daily, such as turning on two-step verification, not sharing one-time codes, and only using official apps or trusted websites. I tried to keep everything simple and relatable so it’s easy to remember and apply in real life. 

Overall, the session went well and made me realise that teaching basic cybersecurity is more about awareness than technical knowledge. Even small habits can make a big difference, especially for people who are not very familiar with technology.
