Basic Cyber Awareness for Parents — Speaker Pointers

Slide 1 - Stay Safe Online
“Today I just want to share a few simple habits that make a big difference online. Cybersecurity is not about being technical. It is mostly about slowing down, noticing red flags, and using a few safe routines.”

Slide 2 - Four Simple Rules
“Here are the main rules. First, use strong passwords, especially for email and banking. Second, be careful with links in emails and texts. Third, keep phones and computers updated. Fourth, if something feels odd, stop and ask. That pause can prevent most problems.”

Slide 3 - Spot Common Scams
“The most common scams are fake emails, fake texts, phone calls, and fake websites. Scammers often create urgency. They may say your account is locked, a package is delayed, or a loved one needs help right away. Red flags are pressure, secrecy, threats, and requests for codes, gift cards, or money transfers.”

Slide 4 - Safe Habits at Home
“A few daily habits help a lot. Turn on two-step verification for important accounts. Never share one-time codes. Use official apps or saved bookmarks for banking and shopping. Back up important files and photos. And be extra careful on public Wi-Fi.”

Slide 5 - What to Do If Something Feels Wrong
“If you think you clicked something bad, do not panic. Stop clicking. Contact the company or person using a phone number or website you already trust. Change passwords if needed. If bank details were involved, call the bank immediately. The main idea is this: stopping early is smart.”

Closing
“The goal is not to be perfect. The goal is to notice warning signs and slow down before acting. If a message pushes urgency, asks for private information, or wants money, it is okay to pause and double-check.”
